# Model Comparison — Opus 4.8 vs Haiku 4.5 for PDA Extraction

_Generated by `experiments/compare_models.py` on 2026-06-05. Sample of 20 PDFs._

> **Decision (2026-06-05): use pure Opus 4.8 for the full corpus.** The comparison
> below shows Haiku is ~81% cheaper but systematically wrong on the study-critical
> fields (grant/denial flags, denial reason, and the `needs_review` audit signal).
> For a research dataset where those fields are the analysis, the ~$100 saving does
> not justify the risk, so the production run uses Opus 4.8 (`pda/extract.py`).

## Methodology

Each PDF was extracted twice with the **identical** system prompt, `record_pda_report` tool, and JSON schema from `pda/extract.py`. The only differences between the two runs are the model and the params Haiku does not accept:

- **Opus** (`claude-opus-4-8`): adaptive thinking + `effort: high` (the production config).
- **Haiku** (`claude-haiku-4-5`): no thinking, no `effort` (Haiku 4.5 rejects both).

Opus is the **baseline** for the diff — not verified ground truth. A disagreement flags a field for human adjudication against the source PDF; it counts against whichever model is actually wrong.

The sample deliberately exercises the subtle fields most likely to trip a smaller model: legacy low-income/elderly percentages (pre-~2016 reports), multi-county per-capita dollar lists, the appeal date chain, tribal `geo_type`, expedited `N/A`→null, and the denial `granted_*`=false rule.

## Adjudication & analysis (n=20)

Expanding from 10 to 20 PDFs (with batch 2 weighted toward tribal nations, a U.S. territory, legacy appeals, and a 25-county approval) **did not change the conclusion — it strengthened it.** The raw counts overstate meaningful disagreement; classified against the source PDFs:

| Category | Stakes | Verdict at n=20 |
|---|---|---|
| **County-name suffix** (`Nez Perce` vs `Nez Perce County`) | Low | Accounts for **all 79 "missing"/79 "extra"** counties — same counties, formatting only; the Phase-2 FIPS matcher normalizes both. |
| **`source` = `both` vs `per_capita`** | Low (provenance) | Still case-dependent; both models err. Not analytical data. |
| **`needs_review` + `review_note`** | **High** (the audit signal) | **Opus flags 7/20 genuinely ambiguous reports; Haiku flags 0/20.** This is the most consistent and important gap — now clearly systematic (two-phase reports, requested-but-unnamed counties, "UNK"/N/A fields, territory-wide grants). |
| **Grant / request flags** (`granted_ia/pa`, `requested_ia/pa`) | **High** (the core research variable) | Haiku repeatedly **conflates "appears in the per-capita list" with "requested/granted"**: wrong `granted_ia/pa=True` for **Kauai** (HI — assessed at $0.00 but not granted) and the **Hoopa Valley Tribe** (PA-only grant), and over-asserts `requested_ia=True` on the **MO appeal**'s 8 per-capita counties. Verified against the PDFs: Opus correct in each clear case. |
| **`denial_reason`** | **Medium–High** (central to a denials study) | Opus consistently captures the official "denied based on the determination that…" sentence. Haiku lowercases/truncates it, **dropped it entirely (`None`) on the TX appeal**, and grabbed the wrong sentence on the **Kivalina** denial. |
| **Proper-noun typos** | Low (FIPS match) | Opus wrote `Choteau`; PDF says `Chouteau` (Haiku right). Cuts the other way once. |
| **`request_date` on multi-date reports** | Medium | The two-phase OK and MO appeal reports have several dates; the models pick different ones. Genuinely ambiguous — Opus flagged both `needs_review`. |

**The numbers that feed the analysis still agree perfectly.** Across all 20 PDFs, **zero** disagreements on IA damage counts, every IA percentage, the cost estimates, and the standard per-capita dollar amounts. Two reports (CA-2007, OK-2011) were byte-identical; several others differed only by the cosmetic county suffix.

**Cost held steady** across the larger sample: **~$123 (Opus) vs ~$23 (Haiku)** for the full corpus, ~81% cheaper.

### Recommendation (unchanged, now better-supported)
Haiku reads the **standard report layout's core data** as accurately as Opus at ~⅕ the cost — but its weaknesses are **systematic and land squarely on the variables this project is built around**: the grant/denial outcome, the denial reason, and the low-confidence audit flag.

- **Pure Opus (~$123)** — recommended. Reliable `needs_review` audit trail and correct grant logic on the edge cases. A modest one-time cost consistent with the quality-over-cost priority.
- **Hybrid (~$45)** — Opus on `Denials` + `AppealDenials` + `Other`/tribal (~209 reports, where the grant/denial/denial-reason fields matter most and Haiku errs), Haiku on bulk standard `MajorDisaster` / `Expedited` approvals (~1,169).
- **Pure Haiku (~$23)** — not advised; it would silently corrupt grant flags and drop denial reasons with no `needs_review` signal to catch it.
