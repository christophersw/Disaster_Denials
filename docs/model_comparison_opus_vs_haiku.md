# Model Comparison — Opus 4.8 vs Haiku 4.5 for PDA Extraction

_Generated by `experiments/compare_models.py` on 2026-06-05. Sample of 10 PDFs._

## Methodology

Each PDF was extracted twice with the **identical** system prompt, `record_pda_report` tool, and JSON schema from `pda/extract.py`. The only differences between the two runs are the model and the params Haiku does not accept:

- **Opus** (`claude-opus-4-8`): adaptive thinking + `effort: high` (the production config).
- **Haiku** (`claude-haiku-4-5`): no thinking, no `effort` (Haiku 4.5 rejects both).

Opus is the **baseline** for the diff — not verified ground truth. A disagreement flags a field for human adjudication against the source PDF; it counts against whichever model is actually wrong.

The sample deliberately exercises the subtle fields most likely to trip a smaller model: legacy low-income/elderly percentages (pre-~2016 reports), multi-county per-capita dollar lists, the appeal date chain, tribal `geo_type`, expedited `N/A`→null, and the denial `granted_*`=false rule.

## Adjudication of key disagreements (checked against source PDFs)

The raw counts above overstate the *meaningful* disagreement. Classifying the 13 report-field and 26 county-field differences against the actual PDFs:

| Category | What it is | Stakes | Who's right |
|---|---|---|---|
| **County-name suffix** | Haiku writes `Nez Perce County`, Opus writes `Nez Perce`. This accounts for **all 26 "missing"/26 "extra" counties** — both models found the *same* counties. | Low — the Phase-2 FIPS fuzzy-matcher normalizes either form. | Neither; inconsistent formatting only. |
| **`source` = `both` vs `per_capita`** | Most county-field diffs. Opus marks a county `both` when it reads it as named in the narrative *and* the per-capita list; Haiku marks `per_capita`. | Low — `source` is provenance metadata, not analytical data. | Case-dependent. Correct for Hawaii (named) where Opus is right; for MT/OK the narrative gives only a *count* ("12 counties"), so `per_capita` (Haiku) is arguably right. Both err. |
| **`denial_reason` completeness/casing** | Opus returns the full sentence; Haiku lowercases and sometimes truncates to the clause (e.g. the CO appeal). | Low–medium — free text. | **Opus** (more complete). |
| **`needs_review` + `review_note`** | On the 3 genuinely ambiguous reports (MT mixed Reservation/County wording; OK two-phase IA-denied/PA-granted; 2007 requested-but-unnamed counties) Opus sets `needs_review=True` with an insightful note. Haiku flags **none**. | **High** — this is the audit signal that lets you trust the dataset. | **Opus** decisively. |
| **Montana `Chouteau`** | Opus wrote `Choteau`; PDF says **`Chouteau`**. | Low (FIPS match). | **Haiku** — Opus made a proper-noun typo. |
| **Hoopa Valley tribal report** | Haiku set `granted_ia=True`; the PDF grants **PA only**, so it should be `False`. Both models mis-mapped the non-standard `$83.85` per-capita line to different fields. | **High** — `granted_ia` is the core denied-vs-granted research variable. | **Opus** on the grant flag (correct `False`); both imperfect on the per-capita placement. |

**The numbers that feed the analysis agreed perfectly.** Across all 10 PDFs there were **zero** disagreements on IA damage counts (destroyed/major/minor/affected), the IA percentages (insured, poverty, SSI/SNAP, ownership, unemployment, age, disability, ICC), the cost estimates, and the standard per-capita dollar amounts. Haiku reads the standard report layout's core data as accurately as Opus.

## Analysis & recommendation

**Where Haiku is fine:** the bulk of the corpus — standard `MajorDisaster` / `Denials` reports — where the core numeric fields are read identically to Opus at **~82% lower cost**.

**Where Haiku is weaker, and it matters for this project:**
1. **No self-auditing.** Haiku returned `needs_review=False` on every report, including the three Opus correctly flagged as ambiguous. For a research dataset you intend to trust, losing the low-confidence flag is the most consequential gap.
2. **Edge-case grant logic.** On the unusual tribal report Haiku got `granted_ia` wrong — and the denied-vs-granted outcome is the central variable of the study.
3. **Free-text completeness** (`denial_reason`) is slightly better on Opus.

These weaknesses concentrate in exactly the analytically-critical and non-standard reports.

**Options:**
- **Pure Opus (~$123):** best quality, reliable `needs_review` audit trail, correct grant flags on edge cases. A modest one-time cost that matches the stated "quality over cost" priority.
- **Pure Haiku (~$23):** 82% cheaper, core numbers solid, but no audit flag and demonstrable edge-case errors — risky for the grant/denial variable.
- **Hybrid (~$45):** Opus on the analytically-critical and non-standard reports — `Denials` + `AppealDenials` + `Other`/tribal (~209 reports) — and Haiku on the bulk standard `MajorDisaster` / `Expedited` approvals (~1,169). Captures most of the savings while putting the stronger model where errors would hurt the research.

**Recommendation:** given the research question hinges on the grant/denial outcome and Haiku both errs on that flag and never flags its own low-confidence rows, prefer **pure Opus** unless budget is a hard constraint, with **hybrid** as the cost-conscious alternative. Pure Haiku is not advised for this dataset.
